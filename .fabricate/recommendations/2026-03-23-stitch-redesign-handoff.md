# gKestral Stitch Redesign Handoff

## Status

Direct Stitch MCP execution is not available in this session, so this file is a handoff package for the next Stitch-enabled pass.

The current website failed because it felt:

- generic instead of category-defining
- too card-heavy and boxed-in
- not premium enough
- not emotionally persuasive enough
- too much like a product memo translated into HTML

The redesign should feel like a serious launch site for a new desktop category.

## Core Positioning

### Primary headline

`The power of Gemini on your desktop, doing your work.`

### Strategic framing

`gKestral is the Gemini-native desktop workbench for a new era of work.`

### Product promise

`A desktop workbench for information, documents, visuals, and execution.`

### Product truth

This is not just a research tool.

This is not just a coding tool.

This is not another AI website.

This is a desktop workbench that helps people turn scattered information into finished work.

## What The Site Must Communicate

1. Gemini belongs on the desktop, not just in a browser tab.
2. The product helps manage information, create documents, reason across visuals, and execute work.
3. Coding is part of the execution layer, not the whole identity.
4. The audience is broader than developers:
   researchers, students, founders, operators, analysts, builders.
5. This is a product with market and investor relevance, not just a concept demo.

## Desired Visual Direction

### Overall feeling

- dark mode by default
- premium
- cinematic
- calm but powerful
- less dashboard, more product manifesto
- more Apple launch-page discipline than SaaS template
- more visual rhythm, fewer repetitive cards

### Avoid

- generic startup gradients
- too many equal-weight boxes
- cluttered UI mockups
- loud “AI” visual clichés
- overexplaining
- internal strategy language

### Lean into

- strong typography hierarchy
- large negative space
- asymmetrical but controlled composition
- one standout hero product scene
- elegant section pacing
- restrained accent color use

## Recommended Site Structure

### 1. Hero

Goal:
Make visitors immediately feel that this is a serious Gemini desktop product.

Required content:

- eyebrow:
  `Gemini-Native Desktop Workbench`
- headline:
  `The power of Gemini on your desktop, doing your work.`
- subhead:
  `Manage information, create documents, work with visuals, and execute real tasks in one place.`
- 2 CTA buttons:
  `See The Product`
  `See The Market Case`

Hero visual direction:

- not a fake dashboard full of tiny unreadable boxes
- instead show one elegant desktop workbench scene
- should suggest:
  - incoming information
  - Gemini synthesis
  - document or work artifact output
  - action/execution layer
- feel like a desktop product, not a web admin panel

### 2. Value Strip

Four concise pillars:

- `Information Management`
- `Document Power`
- `Visual Reasoning`
- `Execution Layer`

Each should have one short sentence only.

### 3. Product Story

Headline:

`Desktop work is still fragmented. The models are ready. The product layer is not.`

Body:

People already use AI for research, writing, planning, visuals, and coding, but they still bounce across tabs, files, websites, and disconnected tools. gKestral exists to bring that work into one serious desktop surface.

Support block:

- one workspace instead of scattered tabs
- documents as outputs, not separate chores
- visuals, files, and context in the same loop

### 4. Product Capabilities

Headline:

`Not another chat window. A desktop surface for serious work.`

Four capability areas:

- `Work from real context`
- `Turn information into documents`
- `Use visuals as first-class input`
- `Execute when the work calls for it`

Tone:
high confidence, minimal copy, strong hierarchy

### 5. Who It Serves

Headline:

`Built for people whose work starts with context and ends with output.`

Audience blocks:

- `Researchers and students`
- `Founders and operators`
- `Builders and analysts`

### 6. Market Case

Headline:

`Powerful models exist, but everyday work is still split across too many surfaces.`

Need to communicate:

- AI product winners will be defined by user experience, not raw model access
- Gemini is unusually strong for long-context, multimodal, research-heavy desktop work
- desktop remains the natural home for files, documents, screenshots, and execution
- there is room for a serious new work product here

### 7. Support / Investor Frame

Headline:

`A product case designed for users, partners, and early believers.`

Subsections:

- `For the market`
- `For ecosystem support`
- `For investor conversations`

This section should feel sharper and more premium than corporate.

### 8. Closing Statement

Headline:

`The Gemini-native workbench for a new era of work.`

Supporting line:

`Information, documents, visuals, and execution in one desktop product.`

## Preferred Copy Blocks

### Short elevator copy

`gKestral brings the power of Gemini onto the desktop for people who need to manage information, create documents, work with visuals, and get real work done.`

### Slightly longer copy

`gKestral is a Gemini-native desktop workbench built for turning scattered information into finished work. It brings research, documents, visuals, and execution into one serious product instead of forcing users to live across tabs, tools, and disconnected workflows.`

### Market-facing copy

`The next important AI products will not win by model access alone. They will win by packaging intelligence into products people trust for real objectives. gKestral is designed for that shift.`

## Stitch Prompt

Use this as the core Stitch prompt.

```markdown
Design a premium dark-mode desktop landing page for a product called gKestral.

This is not a generic SaaS site and not a developer-only coding tool. It is a serious Gemini-native desktop workbench for information, documents, visuals, and execution.

The mood should feel premium, modern, cinematic, and confident. Avoid looking like a card-heavy AI dashboard template. Use bold editorial typography, generous spacing, a disciplined dark palette, elegant gradients, and one standout hero composition that feels like a desktop product launch.

The page should communicate that gKestral brings the power of Gemini onto the desktop and helps users do real work: manage information, create documents, reason across visuals, and execute tasks when needed.

Primary audience:
- researchers and students
- founders and operators
- builders and analysts

Core message:
"The power of Gemini on your desktop, doing your work."

Required sections:

1. Hero section
- Eyebrow: Gemini-Native Desktop Workbench
- Headline: The power of Gemini on your desktop, doing your work.
- Subhead: Manage information, create documents, work with visuals, and execute real tasks in one place.
- Primary CTA: See The Product
- Secondary CTA: See The Market Case
- Include a premium desktop-product visual, not a generic analytics dashboard

2. Value strip with four pillars
- Information Management
- Document Power
- Visual Reasoning
- Execution Layer

3. Story section
- Headline: Desktop work is still fragmented. The models are ready. The product layer is not.
- Short supporting copy explaining that users still bounce across tabs, files, websites, and disconnected tools

4. Product section
- Headline: Not another chat window. A desktop surface for serious work.
- Four capability blocks:
  - Work from real context
  - Turn information into documents
  - Use visuals as first-class input
  - Execute when the work calls for it

5. Audience section
- Headline: Built for people whose work starts with context and ends with output.
- Three audience blocks:
  - Researchers and students
  - Founders and operators
  - Builders and analysts

6. Market section
- Headline: Powerful models exist, but everyday work is still split across too many surfaces.
- Include concise blocks for Why Gemini, Why Desktop, and Why Now

7. Support section
- Headline: A product case designed for users, partners, and early believers.
- Subsections:
  - For the market
  - For ecosystem support
  - For investor conversations

8. Closing section
- Headline: The Gemini-native workbench for a new era of work.
- Supporting line: Information, documents, visuals, and execution in one desktop product.

Design notes:
- dark mode default
- strong visual hierarchy
- fewer but better sections
- avoid repetitive equal-sized cards
- use asymmetry and pacing
- typography should feel premium and bold
- keep copy concise and high-impact
- make the product feel bigger than a research tool and broader than a coding tool
```
